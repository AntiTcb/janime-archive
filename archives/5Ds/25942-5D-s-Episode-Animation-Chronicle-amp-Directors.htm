    Let me see if I can name these off.

    The sakuga directors involved in each of these examples are as follow:
    1. Ken'ichi Hara
    Did sakuga for Duel Monsters and for the GX openings (1-4.) Him, Takahiro Kagami, and Kazunori Takahashi were among the best DM sakuga.

    2. Choi Hoon Chel
    Korean outsourcer sakuga director. Probably did some tweener stuff in DM and GX.

    3. Shin'ichirou Minami
    Did some stuff really really late into DM--like towards the Memory Arc. The first ep he did was 201 and the last he did was 224.

    4. Ichizo Kobayashi
    I believe he did some DM stuff, off the top of my head. Can't give any specific numbers.

    5. Yuuji Kokai
    Don't recall his name from any DM credits, but he is in charge of mecha design for 5D's.

    6. Satoru Minowa
    The episodes on this part of the list are actually three different people with two people working together. Ep 46 are by two (I believe) Korean guys by the names 李敬秀 and 朴致萬.     Perhaps we should, ah, not rank the animation so much as "sort" and categorize it? Hands down, Hara has the most appealing animation style, and few will disagree that Episode 40 had the, er... least appealing animation, but anything in-between would be highly debatable. Personally, I happen to think 3 and 5 have some of the loveliest animation, though the OP would seem to disagree. Why don't we play nice and not let this devolve into an animation-quality argument, shall we?

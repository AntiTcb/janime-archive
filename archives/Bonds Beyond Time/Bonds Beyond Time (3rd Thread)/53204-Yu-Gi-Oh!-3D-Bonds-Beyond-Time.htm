    General Movie Information

    [Q] What is the Yu-Gi-Oh! 10th Anniversary Movie?

    [A] A 49 minute 3D Movie was made by NAS to celebrate the 10th anniversary of the launch of the NAS Yu-Gi-Oh! anime.
    ______

    [Q] This is an OVA, right?

    [A] Wrong. While the project was initially announced to be an OVA, it was later revealed to be a feature length movie. And with its release in movie theaters, it is classified as a movie, not an OVA, which is direct-to-DVD release.
    ______

    [Q] When was the movie released?

    [A]
    • Initial theater release in Japan: 2010 January 23
    • Encore Run release in Japan: 2011 February 26 - March 18
    • USA release in Select Theaters: 2011 February 26-27, March 5-6
    • European release: 2011 Spring (UK)
    • Japanese DVD Release: 2011 June 15
    • English (US) DVD Release: Unknown - To Be Added.
    • English (Europe) DVD Release: 2011 May 30

    [A] The movie is 49 minutes long.

    [Q] In what material was the movie promoted? Where can I find them?

    [A] It was promoted in ads (eyecatches) right before 5D's episodes. The first wave featured special clips of Yugi, Judai and Yusei with their signature monsters. The second wave of eyecatches included clips from the movie and the voice actors, as well as featuring cards which would be used in the movie.

    The movie was also featured in various V-Jump issues, Sakiyomi JanBANG!, Ene!, AxA, FUJIWARA (not the GX character) and Anison Plus+, as well as the official website for the movie and several news sites. After the movie's release, 5D's featured clips of the movie in the episode OPs/EDs, lasting from episodes 093 to 098.

    The English version of the movie was being promoted at the 2010 San Diego Comic Con, and a 6 minute preview was shown in 3D at the 2010 World Championships in August 2010. An artist has made a chalk drawing featuring the three protagonists, which is also an advertisement for Toonzaki, 4Kids' online channel.

    You can find the V-Jump issues on Janime, the CCGN - Channel 8 News subforum.

    [Q] When do the movie's events take place?

    [A] The movie visits several periods of the Yu-Gi-Oh! timeline.

    Distant Future: Paradox's time period. Shown during the explanation of his motives.
    5D's period: Presumably after Bruno's introduction and before the WRGP.
    Post-GX period: Judai is seen in Venice.
    DM period: Certainly post-Duelist Kingdom, possibly post-Battle City. Pegasus is seen attending a tournament in Domino City which Yugi and Sugoroku also attend.
    ______

    [Q] Does the movie establish the timeline between the three series?

    [A] The movie and the 10th Anniversary Animation Book both confirm that all three series take place in the same universe.
    ______

    [Q] Which characters appear in the movie?

    [A] The movie features Yugi, Judai and Yusei going up against the movie's antagonist, Paradox. The following characters appear from the respective eras:

    5D's: Jack, Aki, Crow, Rua, Ruka (Flashback characters: Ushio, Kiryu, Rex Godwin, Rudger, Sherry, Bruno and others)
    GX: Daitokuji, Pharaoh (the cat), Yubel
    DM: Sugoroku, Pegasus

    The cast members of Sakiyomi JanBANG! make a cameo as well. They are Akkiina, Zaripai and Amepai.
    ______

    [Q] Are there any pairings in the movie?

    [A] No, there are no (new) pairings in the movie, but Judai and Yubel are together, as the movie Judai is the post-GX one.
    ______

    [Q] Who is Paradox?

    [A] Paradox is an exclusive character who made his debut in the movie, and is the movie's antagonist. His design was overseen by Takahiro Kagami. However, the screenwriter of the movie who also oversees the Series Structure for 5D's as of Episode 027, Shin Yoshida, stated that those who wish to "find out Paradox's true character" should watch the future 5D's episodes. This piece of information was stated in the 10th Anniversary Animation Book's Movie Staff Interview section.

    Note: As of Episode 135 of 5D's, Paradox's identity has been confirmed, and Episode 146 confirmed his place in the series.
    ______

    [Q] What is Paradox's motive? What is his identity? (Spoilers!)

    [A]
    Spoiler: Movie only explanation
    Paradox claims he has come from a future where the world is destroyed (he didn't state by what), and he intends to save the hopeless future. He is trying to make the best history a possibility. The Japanese trailer's tagline makes a mention of "Orders to kill the Creator of Duel Monsters", but it is not known if Paradox is the one to be given this order, or the identity of the person who gives out the order. (The tagline was omitted from the English preview shown at World Championship 2010.) The order is not brought up in the Original Film. The tagline's meaning has been confirmed in Episode 146 of 5D's.

    Paradox wants to kill Pegasus in order to change history. The Animation Book states he discovered the power of Duel Monsters in his own time period, so he has come to the protagonists' time.

    According to the movie's official site, Paradox's identity is draped in mystery. He did not reveal what or who he is in the movie itself.


    Spoiler: Extended explanation (5D's SPOILERS!)
    The distant future and Paradox's identity were expanded upon in Episodes 134 and 135 of 5D's. It was revealed that excessive Synchro Summoning led to humanity evolving at a way too fast speed, which ended in an army of Machine Emperors wiping out mankind. In the very end, the greed of humanity reached its peak, resulting in the Momentum Reactors becoming uncontrollable all over the planet, exploding simultaneously and wiping out humans entirely.

    An older Paradox, who is allied with Zone and who appears to be an old Bruno, find Aporia in a dome, the last human remaining on Earth. They formed an alliance, performing endless experiments in order to change their bleak future. However, Bruno and Paradox die of old age, and eventually Aporia as well. Zone has been preserved in a machine, who has made a promise to the dying Aporia that he will change the future. The old Bruno, whose real name is Antinomy, has asked Zone if he could be his servant in changing the future.

    In Episode 146, Zone himself states he sent Paradox back in time to eliminate the root of Duel Monsters.

    [Q] Sin? Malefic?

    [A] Malefic is the English (TCG) name for the Sin monsters.

    Sin is because they're monsters of the new (Shin) world to come, a world built on sin (Shin).

    A number of kanjis can be read as "Shin" (which is the Japanese pronunciation of "Sin"), including 真 truth, 新 new and 神 divine/kami. Masahiro Hikokubo, the Duel Writer for the Movie, stated that the "Sin" theme is a clever combination of "sin" (罪 tsumi) and "new" (新 atarashii).

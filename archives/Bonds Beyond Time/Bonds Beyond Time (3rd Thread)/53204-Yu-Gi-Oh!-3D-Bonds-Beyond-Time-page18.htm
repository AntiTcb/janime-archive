    Re: Yu-Gi-Oh! 3D Bonds Beyond Time

    Just got back from Bonds Beyond Time. I can't really add too much extra so I'll mostly talk about my experience there rather than the movie.

    First off, $8.25 for popcorn & water!??!?! D:< At least I got the Malefic Red-Eyes/Manga sampler, though. The lady at the snack counter wanted to see this movie so badly, but sh has to work today & tomorrow. Guess she'll just have to wait for the DVD.

    Anyway, went inside the theater, and found out there were only about 20 or less people there. I'll just assume the large crowd of people that wanted to see this movie saw it last weekend.

    Then I saw previews for Cars 2
    ...
    & Kung Fu Panda 2
    ...
    & Mars Needs Moms
    ...
    & Smurfs 3D D:< D:< D:<

    Then the recap started. If someone who never saw YGO before went to this movie & saw that, they would probably go, "WTF am I watching?!?!?!"

    Then the actual movie. Just. AMAZING.
    Pure.
    ****ing.
    Awesome.

    It was hard to be surprised from this movie since I read all of the spoiler summaries, but DAMN! The 3D combined with stellar animation practically had me in eye-gasms for the entire 50 mins. And as others have said, the music was outstanding for 4KIDS standards, complete with silence for all of the necessary parts. Still might have been nice to hear the original OST's, but I still like their music selection. A LOT. The DM Yugioh music had me SO nostalgic. Nice touch.

    There were 2 things I didn't like, though:

    1) The parts before Yusei went back in time felt a little TOO fast-paced. If they had just expanded upon some of the earlier scenes, it could have come a lot closer to feature-length time.
    2) Paradox was made to be a typical 2-D 4KIDS style villain. They did it in the recap too with Pegasus, Viper, and "The ruthless Seto Kaiba."
    Paradox even came complete with a 4KIDS-style censor. (Paradox: "If you lose a Malefic Game, you lose your soul!" )

    But these were still only minor issues. I still thoroughly enjoyed the movie.

    Then I watched the credits roll to the 3 themes, saw them display the 10th anniversary icon one last time, and then it was over.

    After that, though, I heard these 2 guys about my age (I'm 19, btw) talk about the movie:

    #1: I can't wait for this to come out on DVD.
    #2: But it was only 45 minutes.
    #1: I know, but I loved it.

    I couldn't agree more. I was so happy to hear that from them.

    Bottom line, I really enjoyed the entire ordeal. I loved it so much.

    Re: Yu-Gi-Oh! 3D Bonds Beyond Time

    Someone asked me what I thought about the movie. Ha ha, I would like to share my feeling as a regular fan not Janime here ^__^. Well, I have just watched it this morning with my family. I have to say, "Impressive!," compared to what I have seen during the past 10 years. I started watching Yu Gi Oh since 1997 (Toei Production). Wow, 3D is truly amazing. Some scenes based on old pictures are still rough, but they did a pretty good job though.

    Pictures - It's of course the best of all times based on the latest 3D technology. It is also based on the original Japanese production, so the pictures are pretty beautiful, colorful, and better than the prior movie, Pyramid of Light.

    Duel Contents - It's truly an intermediate-expert level. There are many cards played at different timing, and you still need to exercise your brain a bit to follow all effects. I like how it's nicely done so that we just don't watch without thinking.

    Environment - It is a limited show in theater and only available on a time per day during the weekend in my city, so there were many kids and family getting together in the morning. The theater was crowded. Many kids were cheering, playing Yu Gi Oh cards (even checking real cards while watching the movie), and laughing. For me, it seems so fun for a lively movie. Glad that nobody knew I was Janime ^^".

    Dub - I know that they might change some scripts to match the US market. However, in my personal view, I think it isn't bad at all (compared to the dub in regular series). English isn't my native language, so I can't comment much. I might not get all lines correctly, but the best line from the movie is from Yugi, "Synchro What!?". Ha ha, it made all audiences laugh repeatedly as Yugi doesn't have any idea about synchro.

    Storyboard - It takes about 15 mins for them to give a perview of the 3 main heros, the next 15-2 mins for the story, and the remaining time for the duel. For the timeframe, it is okay for me. I think that they can make this a better movie by adding more story =)

    Well, you have a day left to watch this 3D movie in theater. It's worth to pay and watch! I strongly encourage you to get out and watch it ^0^.

    - I kind of wanted to smack Yusei for not taking the Stardust Dragon when Paradox held it up in front of his face when he was right besides of him lol

    Re: Yu-Gi-Oh! 3D Bonds Beyond Time

    Now I can finally write a review of the movie, long after everybody else has already seen it and got over it. This review is dedicated to whoever it was who, against a proud Konami/4Kids tradition of protesting that subbed YGO can't be released outside Japan because blablabla&etc, stood up and said "Not this time!" and melted the subbed version onto the UK DVD with the sheer power of their will. I don't think it's been mentioned yet, but the official subbed title was "Super Fusion! Bonds Beyond Time and Space".

    Well, you certainly can't say there's a wasted moment in this movie. It's pretty much balls-to-the-walls YGO-ness. The duel is a lot of fun, and the lack of turns doesn't show at all, because each turn is so significant and cinematic. The Sin deck is a conceptually-interesting archetype and Sin World makes a truly beautiful setpiece for the battle. As does Venice in the earlier scene, and there's clearly no reason for Judai to be in Italy other than for the picturesqueness. Works for me. Junk Gardna and Neos Knight are surprisingly bland-looking monsters considering they're two of the protagonists' shiny new cards though.

    Paradox is, needless to say, the perfect villain for this movie. At the anime's tenth anniversary, he represents all those who despise children wasting their time and money on card games, those who don't see the point. The heroes represent the freedom to choose to duel. Everything about Paradox is a paradox, from his use of Duel Monsters to destroy Duel Monsters to his embrace of Sin World to stop the sins of mankind, and even in his costume which seems to be made of the clothes of various past villains. In a perfect world, he'd have been given more detailed backstory. Even in a non-perfect world, we should have at least got a few more lines explaining why he needed to steal powerful cards from history and why he needed to kill Pegasus at that point. Just because we fans usually have to assume some ******** about 'duel energy' solving everything doesn't mean we should.

    There's been some complaints about the soundtrack, and I thought it was surprisingly subdued up until the duel. From then on though, it kicks ass. Ho-ly crap, dat Light and Dark segment. Ditto Bonds of the Three. I can even bear the inferior GX version of Passionate Duelists in this situation. On the level of the fact that they had three series, all with amazing soundtracks, to choose tracks from, then yes -- it's disappointing. They should have used at least one track from DM, considering it has the best soundtrack of all three series. But if you go in blind and forget that they could have used stuff like Fang of Critias and Wrath of God, then it's hard to be disappointed. Besides, it looks like now we fans can replace the soundtrack to our hearts' content (your Duel of Friendship is a perfect fit, Goesasu).

    I suppose if I were to be a bit less loving I'd have to point out that 50 minutes is a pathetic running time (even if it keeps things fresh), and the meeting of the three protagonists goes smoothly without any interesting conflict or surprises at all. If this had been a full-length movie, it would have been the ultimate love-letter to YGO fans. As it is, it doesn't really have the content to reach that high category.

    I haven't watched the dub movie. I might do at some point, but I can make a pretty accurate guess of what it's like, especially considering the clips I've seen of it: it takes the usual current 4Kids dub route of keeping the plot about the same but steadfastly refusing to be a direct translation, it has a strange habit of taking itself too seriously when it shouldn't and not taking itself seriously enough when it should, its soundtrack sets the scene but isn't something you'd care to have on your iPod, its voice-acting is high quality but the dialogue is the definition of cringeworthy and lets the actors down, and it has two primary audiences -- children aged 7-12 and members of PlaytheDamnCard. Unfortunately, [?] I am neither of those things.

    Maybe the protagonists of the next three anime will meet up someday too. Looking at Zexal, I really doubt I'll be around for that.
    ~ The Resident Evil Club on Janime ~

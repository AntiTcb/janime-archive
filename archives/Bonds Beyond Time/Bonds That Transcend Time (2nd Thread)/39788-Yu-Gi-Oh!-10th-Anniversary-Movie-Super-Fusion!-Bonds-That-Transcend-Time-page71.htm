    Re: Yu-Gi-Oh! 10th Anniversary Movie ~Super Fusion! Bonds That Transcend Time~

    Not so awesome from the standpoint from somebody who wants to make sure that new mediums get the respect they deserve. 4Kid's currently using the same tactic it used for Kinnikuman Nisei(which avoided being a total letdown only because the joke alterations, personalities, and backgrounds of the characters were for the most part, portrayed fairly well), and is taking a page from Plastic Cow's mutilation of Duel Masters. They're relying primarily on bad puns and horrible references that, while for all intents and purposes may be remotely funny black humor, are a mockery and disgrace to the source material. Especially when it comes to the plot, the characters, their personalities, and their atmospheres. There weren't like this with Pokemon or early YGO. Atem's saga, despite the massive number of alterations, had VAs who hit the characters' personas and atmospheres well for the most part. This facebook account has Atem making jokes that not even Judai's limited intelligence would laugh at.

    I'm going to say this. Korea has a tendency to try too hard and generally has elementary school level reading atmosphere for its game/anime/manga industry. Rockman Online made me remember that, and even IT didn't destroy character personas and settings to the extent 4Kids does at times. Had YGO been dying out right now, I assure you that this stunt would've gotten flak for breaking the fourth wall to mutilate the personalities of the characters and gather attention in horrible. The only reason it's not gathering flak is because YGO sells well, and YGO abridged is a good piece of black humor ala MAD movies for anybody who knows what they're making fun of. 4Kids, however, has shown time and time again that they don't give a shit about the source material(much like the current Japan), nevering bothering to experience the stuff they're licensing before they actually license things. The chances of them understanding the meaning that the abridged series holds in peoples' hearts are next to 0. They're saying yes just because they know its popular.

    Generally, I don't dislike 4Kids for their changes or their bad dubbing. I hate it for the attitude they hold towards the industry. They're a company that personifies everything wrong with the anime market, just that it's not in Japan and is on the other end of the spectrum compared to the fanservice-laden animation industry of today. And this facebook stunt makes me see once again just how greedy they are. If they were fans, I'd marvel at how much they know about the subculture generated by the source material. But they aren't, so this just makes me rage.

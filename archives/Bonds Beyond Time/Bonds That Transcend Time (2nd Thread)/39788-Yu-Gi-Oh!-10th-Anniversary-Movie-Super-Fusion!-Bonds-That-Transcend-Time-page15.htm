    __DUEL__

    Interpretation of the rules

    They duel with 4000 LP
    Paradox starts with 5 cards, the three start with 5 cards each (15 altogether)
    The three share a field, graveyard, and LP

    1. Opponent's first turn
    Duelists: Player A, opponent

    2. Own first turn (Player A's first turn)
    Duelists: Player A, opponent
    (Players B and C don't draw a card)

    3. Opponent's second turn
    Duelists: Player A, opponent

    4. Own second turn (Player B's first turn)
    Duelists: Player B, opponent
    (Players A and C don't draw)

    5. Opponent's third turn
    Duelists: Player B, opponent

    6. Own third turn (Player C's first turn)
    duelists: Player C, opponent
    (Players A and B don't draw)

    7. (you get it by now) Opponent's fourth turn
    Duelists: Player C, opponent

    8. Own fourth turn (Player A's second turn)
    Duelists: Player A, opponent
    (Players B and C don't draw)

    After the duelists start dueling, they duel together (i.e. it's not a Battle Royale) and face one opponent. That's what the duel consists of
    The duelists can't check each other's cards, deck, face-down cards, graveyard and cards removed from play, but they can check cards they use together (all three have control over the monsters)
    The duelists who aren't dueling can activate cards they have face-down, in the graveyard or removed from play during their partner's turn, but they can't use cards from their hand or deck
    The duelists who aren't dueling can't bring cards from their hands or deck into the duel at that time. The three can't use those cards together (the cards of the others can't be summoned, placed or used as a cost)
    (i.e. Yuusei can't, for example, summon a monster from Yugi's hand during his turn)

    The player whose LP hits zero will fall down to his death.

    duelists: Player C, opponent

    Duelists: Player C, opponent

    8. Own fourth turn (Player A's second turn)
    Duelists: Player A, opponent
    (Players B and C don't draw)

    After the duelists start dueling, they duel together (i.e. it's not a Battle Royale) and face one opponent. That's what the duel consists of
    The duelists can't check each other's cards, deck, face-down cards, graveyard and cards removed from play, but they can check cards they use together (all three have control over the monsters)
    The duelists who aren't dueling can activate cards they have face-down, in the graveyard or removed from play during their partner's turn, but they can't use cards from their hand or deck
    The duelists who aren't dueling can't bring cards from their hands or deck into the duel at that time. The three can't use those cards together (the cards of the others can't be summoned, placed or used as a cost)
    (i.e. Yuusei can't, for example, summon a monster from Yugi's hand during his turn)

    The player whose LP hits zero will fall down to his death.
